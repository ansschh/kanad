2020

Kanad was developed in response to the inefficiencies and waste associated with traditional farming methods, as well as the challenges faced by farmers due to climate change and increasing food demand. The creator of Kanad identified the need for a more intelligent and automated system that could optimize water usage, improve crop yields, and suggest appropriate fertilizers and crops based on soil conditions.

Ongoing research

Kanad's journey has been one of progress and innovation, with the development of its IoT-enabled intelligent irrigation system and machine learning algorithms for precision agriculture. The system has shown promising results in reducing water waste and increasing crop yields, with its deep learning models providing accurate predictions for irrigation schedules, crop selection, fertilization, and disease prevention. The team behind Kanad continues to work towards expanding its capabilities and reaching more farmers in need, with a commitment to sustainable agriculture practices and improving food security for all.

Research and development

Kanad: Smart Farming Solution

Kanad is an Internet of Things (IoT)-enabled intelligent irrigation system designed to solve the problem of inefficiency in traditional farming methods. With its smart irrigation feature, farmers can automate and optimize their watering process, using data collected from sensors to make precise predictions for optimal pump operation. Kanad also helps farmers determine the best crops to plant and appropriate fertilizers to use, based on NPK levels, as well as predicting crop disease using CNN. By optimizing agricultural processes, Kanad saves water, energy, and costs, while increasing crop production to meet the growing demand for food.

Who are we?

2050

Kanad's goal by 2050 is to establish itself as a leading solution for precision agriculture, with its deep learning algorithms and Internet of Things (IoT)-enabled system being widely adopted by farmers around the world. The focus should be on further improving the system's accuracy and efficiency, and expanding its capabilities beyond irrigation, crop suggestions, fertiliser suggestions, and disease prediction, to include other important aspects of farming such as pest control and harvesting. Additionally, Kanad should aim to support sustainable agriculture practices, which can help mitigate the impact of farming on the environment, while also improving crop quality and yield. Ultimately, the aim should be to help farmers meet the growing demand for food and sustainably manage our natural resources.
